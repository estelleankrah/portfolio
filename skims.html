The Brief

As part of a live industry brief at UAL London College of Fashion, I took on the role of Brand Guardian for SKIMS, Kim Kardashian's shapewear and loungewear brand. The task was to analyse SKIMS' brand identity, map its stakeholder landscape, audit its existing crisis history, and develop a structured crisis communications strategy across two live crisis scenarios.

SKIMS launched in 2019 as a size-inclusive shapewear line (originally named 'Kimono' before a cultural appropriation controversy forced the rebrand) and grew to a $1.6 billion business. Its brand DNA is built around diversity, inclusivity, and body positivity, values that made each crisis all the more reputationally sensitive.

My role covered the full crisis lifecycle: identifying the crisis, crafting the immediate response, managing internal and external stakeholders, preparing the media Q&A, and proposing long-term brand strategies to rebuild and protect reputation.

Brand

Brand History & Values

Before managing a crisis, I needed to understand the brand from the inside. SKIMS was founded in 2019 after Kim Kardashian identified a gap in the market for size-inclusive shapewear. Within minutes of launch the collection made $2 million, and it has since expanded into loungewear, underwear, and pyjamas. Its core brand value, diversity and inclusivity, is central to every campaign and every crisis response.

Context

A History of Crisis

SKIMS has navigated three major public crises since its founding. Each one tested a different dimension of the brand's values: cultural sensitivity, ethical manufacturing, and digital responsibility. I mapped this full crisis history as the foundation for developing the response strategy.

Crisis timeline: Kimono controversy (2019), Turkey production controversy (2019), TikTok body distortion (2021)

The Kimono Crisis (June 2019): cultural appropriation backlash, #KimOhNo trending on Twitter, open letter from the Mayor of Kyoto

The Production Controversy (September 2019): SKIMS manufacturing in Turkey amid Armenian Genocide awareness, went viral on Twitter

Crisis Strategy

For each crisis I developed a structured response: immediate social media statement, spokesperson selection (Jens Grade, Co-Founder and Board Director, chosen over Kim Kardashian to depersonalise the response), stakeholder communication plan, and a media Q&A. The strategy prioritised transparency and action over deflection.

Stage 1 crisis solution: social statement, spokesperson briefing, investigation announcement

SWOT analysis: mapping brand strengths and vulnerabilities to inform long-term strategy

Insight

Estelle's Work

What this taught me

Working as Brand Guardian for SKIMS showed me that crisis communications is inseparable from brand strategy. Every response has to be consistent with the brand's stated values, when a brand built on inclusivity misappropriates culture or uses unethical supply chains, the reputational damage is compounded by the hypocrisy. The long-term strategy I proposed centred on proactive transparency: publishing supply chain information, establishing clearer cultural consultation processes, and building media relationships before a crisis hits.
