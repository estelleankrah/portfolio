The Brand

CUT is a purpose-driven sustainable denim brand built around a single mission: using fashion to raise awareness about knife crime in the UK. Developed at UAL London College of Fashion, CUT partnered with Blackhorse Lane Ateliers, East London's most celebrated denim manufacturer, to produce knife-safe denim as both a product and a statement.

The brand's tagline, "WEAR YOUR MORALS.", positions CUT at the intersection of fashion, community and activism, a brand that believes what you wear communicates what you stand for. The campaign targeted a nationwide UK audience, acknowledging that knife crime is not exclusively a London issue but one that resonates across cities and communities.

I led the brand report, press strategy, event production, influencer strategy, social media campaign, and website design for the CUT launch, delivered in partnership with the Foundation for Future London, Catalyst in Communities, Centre for Sustainable Fashion, and UAL London College of Fashion. Online engagement increased 20% through the launch campaign.

The Mission

Estelle's Work

Fashion as Activism

CUT's brand centres around knife crime as a social issue with deep cultural significance in the UK. The campaign was designed not simply to sell jeans but to raise awareness, start conversations, and create community around an issue that disproportionately affects young people. By making the product itself knife-safe, CUT collapsed the distance between fashion and lived experience.

Event Design

Estelle's Work

Event Space Concept

The event space at Blackhorse Lane Ateliers, Coal Drops Yard was designed around the brand's sustainable, community-led identity: exposed brick walls, a living green wall with bar, an indoor tree with curved seating, and denim displayed throughout as both product and art object.

The Launch

22 July 2021, Coal Drops Yard

The CUT launch event was held at Blackhorse Lane Ateliers, Unit 32, Lower Stable Street, Coal Drops Yard on 22 July 2021, 6PM–8PM. The evening featured live music from Zoe Kypri, drinks, food, and a panel debate on knife crime featuring Stephen Barnabis. The event was supported by four institutional partners and was run on behalf of info@projectcut.co.uk.

Estelle's Work

Media Strategy

Press coverage was targeted across national and community titles: Waltham Forest Echo, Sublime Magazine, The Guardian, Kiosk NC1, and BBC Radio London, a deliberately mixed strategy spanning lifestyle press and hyperlocal community journalism to match the brand's dual identity as fashion product and social campaign.

Influencer strategy: Melissa's Wardrobe (197k followers, Stormzy's stylist). Avg 15,000 likes/post. Promoting CUT via #MAKETHECUT and "how to style" content on both her channels and CUT's social platforms.

Consumer and culture moodboard: Free Spirit, Tech Savy, i-D, GQ, The Guardian. CUT's audience is culturally engaged, community-driven, and purpose-led.
