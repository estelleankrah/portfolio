Legal & Admin

Overview

At Tesco I took on a broad operational role that sat at the intersection of legal compliance, financial administration, and team management. Day-to-day responsibilities spanned three core areas: maintaining statutory compliance documentation, running cash office operations, and coordinating checkout colleagues on the shop floor.

Within six weeks of starting I was promoted to Colleague Manager, a recognition of the standards I brought to every part of the role — from the precision of the compliance paperwork to the consistency of how I managed and supported the team around me.

Legal Compliance

A central part of the role was enforcing and documenting Tesco's statutory obligations around age-restricted sales. I administered Challenge 25 checks across the checkout team, ensuring every colleague understood the policy and applied it consistently — logging incidents, flagging gaps in compliance, and keeping records audit-ready at all times.

I also handled the legal compliance documentation for lottery and alcohol sales, two of the highest-risk product categories in retail. This included maintaining the required licensing records, completing the associated paperwork at the correct intervals, and ensuring staff were briefed on their obligations under the relevant regulations. Every document had to be accurate, dated, and filed correctly — there was no margin for error.

Cash Office

I managed cash office operations including cash counting, float reconciliation, and end-of-day paperwork. This required a high level of numerical accuracy and discretion. Any discrepancy had to be identified, investigated, and documented — whether it originated at a till, in a float, or in a safe handover. I kept all cash handling records clean and accountable.

Team Management

Alongside the compliance and cash office work, I managed checkout colleagues directly — coordinating break schedules, covering till gaps, handling escalations, and maintaining a calm, efficient shop floor during busy periods. I was responsible for briefing the team on any compliance updates and for ensuring standards were upheld consistently across every shift.

Being promoted to Colleague Manager meant taking on formal accountability for team performance, which I treated with the same rigour I applied to the documentation side of the role.
