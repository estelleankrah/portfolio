The Final Year Project

For my final year at UAL London College of Fashion, I chose Hanifa as my brand and developed a full 12-month rotational PR and marketing campaign, concluding in a realised exhibition concept: REBIRTH: The Becoming of Hanifa, conceived for the Barbican Centre in London.

The project began with a complete brand audit (brand identity, crisis history, stakeholder mapping, consumer research, competitor analysis) and built to a comprehensive PR plan covering press strategy, social media, partnerships, and event programming across a full year.

The final tactic was the exhibition. REBIRTH traces Anifa Mvuemba's journey from the Democratic Republic of Congo to building one of the most innovative brands in contemporary fashion. The Barbican was chosen as the venue for its cultural weight, its architectural drama, and its history of presenting boundary-pushing Black creative work to global audiences.

I produced the full spatial concept including a ground-level floor plan, 3D architectural visualisations across all rooms, and an Instagram account documenting the planning process in real time.

The Exhibition Concept

REBIRTH takes visitors on a journey through the heritage that shaped Hanifa as a brand: the Congo, the Atlantic crossing, the immigrant experience, the ambition, and the art. Each room in the exhibition corresponds to a chapter in Anifa Mvuemba's story, moving from the landscape of her childhood (sand, water, palm trees, a traditional pirogue boat) through to the modern fashion houses and global stages she has conquered. The exhibition is named in Lingala, Mvuemba's mother tongue, and the word "rebirth" is translated across five African languages on the entrance wall.

Spatial Planning

Ground Level Floor Plan

The ground level floor plan maps the full exhibition layout across the Barbican's circular footprint. The plan organises the visitor journey through four zones:

  • Entrance, marble wall, Lingala text, arrival into Hanifa's world
  • Room 1, heritage rooms with the boat, waterfall and Congolese landscape
  • Exhibition Areas 1–3, fashion, cultural archive, and brand story
  • Giftshop, Hanifa products, exhibition catalogue, and exclusive collaboration pieces
