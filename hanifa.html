The Project

For my Year 3 Research for Independent Project at UAL London College of Fashion (tutor: Lucy Finnegan), I chose to conduct a full brand audit of Hanifa, a global luxury womenswear brand founded by Anifa Mvuemba, a Congolese immigrant who built one of the most innovative brands in contemporary fashion entirely on her own terms.

Hanifa is best known for its 2020 Instagram Live fashion show, where 3D-rendered digital models walked an invisible runway in the brand's pink linen collection, a landmark moment in digital fashion history. With the tagline "For Women Without Limits," the brand speaks to a specific vision of modern luxury: inclusive, culturally rooted, digitally native.

My research covered the full communications ecosystem: internal brand structure, PR strategy, social media performance, consumer profiling, competitor landscape (Andrea Iyamah, Christie Brown, House of CB), pricing analysis, brand ethics, and a sector SWOT. The report ran to 136 pages including primary consumer research.

Brand Identity

For Women Without Limits

Hanifa puts creativity at the heart of its strategy, positioning itself as a global luxury brand that sets new trends while crafting tomorrow's culture responsibly. Founder Anifa Mvuemba emigrated from the Congo to the US and built the brand with no mentor and no funding, an origin story that is inseparable from the brand's identity.

Brand tagline and visual identity: "For Women Without Limits"

Brand positioning statement: global luxury, creative-first, responsible innovation

Foundation

The Founder & PR Structure

Understanding who Anifa Mvuemba is matters for every communications decision the brand makes. She is the creative director, business owner, and most powerful spokesperson Hanifa has. My research also mapped Hanifa's PR representation, Purple PR New York, a global agency with clients including Fenty and DSquared2, took on the brand as of July 2022.

Meet the Founder: Anifa Mvuemba, Congolese-American designer, self-built, no external investment

PR Representation: Purple PR New York, a global luxury agency, Instagram strategy and international brand awareness

Digital Innovation

The Instagram Live Runway

One of the most distinctive elements of my Hanifa research was documenting the brand's groundbreaking use of digital innovation. In 2020, Hanifa staged a virtual fashion show on Instagram Live where 3D-rendered models wore the pink linen collection on an invisible runway, the first of its kind. This became a case study in how independent luxury brands can command global press coverage without a physical show budget.

Research Findings

Estelle's Work

Ethics, Pricing & Market Position

A key finding in my research was the tension between Hanifa's aspirational brand identity and its limited transparency on ethics and sustainability. While the brand positions itself as luxury, it lacks published information about supply chain practices, a vulnerability I flagged as a growing consumer concern in the luxury sector. On pricing, I found that Hanifa had moved significantly upmarket, with dresses ranging from £190 to £2,126, reflecting its growing positioning as an accessible luxury rather than emerging brand.

Brand ethics audit: CSR transparency gap as a key strategic vulnerability

Pricing analysis: £190 entry point to £2,126 eveningwear, positioning between accessible and true luxury
